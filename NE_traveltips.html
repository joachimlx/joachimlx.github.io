Travel tips

NipponExplorer's tips will help you navigate and prepare for traveling to Japan!

1. JR Pass

With a Japan Rail Pass, you can travel unlimitedly on JR trains throughout Japan.

2. Mobile data

For your trip, it's advisable to get a SIM card for your phone or a portable Wi-Fi router, so you can maintain internet connection and facilitate navigation around the city.

3. Onsen - Japanese Hot Springs

Experience a traditional Japanese bath known as onsen. There's nothing better than a relaxing bathing experience in Japan while also immersing yourself in Japanese culture.

4. Ryokan Accommodation

Consider booking a stay at a traditional Japanese inn, called a ryokan. It offers a unique and authentic experience of Japanese lifestyle.

5. Etiquette

It's important to observe Japanese etiquette when traveling to Japan. Adhering to local customs and traditions is crucial. Remember to remove your shoes indoors, be polite and respectful, and avoid talking loudly, especially on trains.

6. Language

Learn a few basic Japanese phrases or get a translation app to better communicate with locals. English proficiency in Japan is generally limited.

7. Temples and Shrines

Exploring Japanese temples and shrines is a key aspect of traveling to Japan. Always remember to respect local customs and behave appropriately during your visit.

8. Seasons

Plan your trip according to the seasons. Spring is the best time to visit Japan, but it's also the busiest with tourists. Summer brings the rainy season (except in Hokkaido), while winter is quiet and ideal for visiting mountains for activities like skiing.

9. Food

Japan offers a variety of traditional dishes such as sushi, ramen, tempura, and okonomiyaki. Even if you're not a fan of seafood, you can still enjoy your time in Japan!

10. Cost of Living

Major cities like Tokyo and Osaka can be expensive, while smaller cities and rural areas tend to be more affordable.
