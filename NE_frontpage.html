Plan a ten-day trip to Japan

In Japan, there is a lot to see, and you're probably wondering how you can experience as much as possible in one trip. With this travel itinerary, you'll be able to plan a diverse journey full of various and unique attractions!

Day 1 and 2: Tokyo
In Tokyo, there are numerous iconic attractions such as Senso-ji Temple, Shibuya Crossing, Tokyo Skytree, and the Imperial Palace. The first few days in Tokyo will allow you to experience these key attractions and gain the most comprehensive insight into Tokyo!

Read more about Tokyo

Day 3: Fuji and Kyoto
The next day, you'll have the opportunity to see the famous Mount Fuji, Japan's most iconic landmark, before continuing your journey towards Kyoto!

Day 4: Kyoto
In Kyoto, it's worth spending two days as you experience old traditions, temples, and shrines in Japan's former imperial capital.

Read more about FujiRead more about Kyoto

Day 5: Nara
In the nearby city of Nara, you'll find the great Buddha statue, Daibutsu, which is one of the eight UNESCO World Heritage Sites in Nara. Nara is also home to Nara Park, where you can meet the polite Japanese deer!

Read more about Nara

Day 6: Osaka
Within a 40-minute journey from Nara, you'll arrive in Osaka, where you can especially enjoy the nightlife amidst bright lights and delicious food.

Day 7: Himeji
In Himeji, you can visit Himeji Castle, which has become a national treasure of Japan and one of UNESCO's World Heritage Sites.

Day 8 and 9: Hiroshima
Within an hour's journey from Himeji, you'll reach Hiroshima, a famous city in Japan due to its historical events. There, you can visit the Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park, which showcases, among other things, the ruins of World War II.

Day 10: Miyajima
The journey is well concluded with a visit to Miyajima, where you'll see Japan's famous attraction, the Itsukushima Shrine's torii gate, which appears to float above the sea during high tide. At the end of the day, you can stay at a traditional Japanese inn.
