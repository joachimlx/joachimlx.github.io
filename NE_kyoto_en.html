KYOTO

Welcome to Kyoto, the former capital of Japan! The city of Kyoto is known for its rich history, magnificent temples, and ancient culture.

History and culture

The narrow streets and traditional buildings of Kyoto's old town offer visitors the opportunity to step back in time. Explore Kyoto's famous temples such as Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion) and Kiyomizu-dera Temple, both of which are UNESCO World Heritage Sites and offer stunning views.

In Kyoto, you can deeply experience traditional Japanese culture. For example, tea ceremonies, wearing kimonos, and strolling along the Philosopher's Path are all experiences worth trying.

Fushimi Inari-Taisha

Fushimi Inari-Taisha is a significant Shinto shrine known for its thousands of red torii gates, creating an impressive pathway up the mountain.

The shrine is dedicated to the Shinto gods of rice and sake. Along the pathway, you'll also encounter hundreds of stone fox statues, which are said to be messengers of Inari.
